# Incubator Fit Review: FORGE (FRG)

> **Draft — for internal review only. Not for publication.**

---

## Verdict

**Strong fit**

---

## Why it fits HACD

FORGE is a strong fit for HACD Stack issuance because:

1. **Credible supply origin is core to the product thesis.** AI compute credits with freely mintable supply are structurally unverifiable. FORGE uses HACD Stack to make FRG supply origin traceable and immutable — this is not decorative; it is the product differentiation.

2. **Burn-on-use mechanics align with HACD's formation model.** Each FRG token leaves the supply permanently when used. Combined with PoW-anchored issuance, this creates a supply model where new FRG requires real cost inputs and consumed FRG cannot be silently re-issued.

3. **200 HACD lot cap is a credible scarcity mechanism.** The cap is structurally enforced by HACD unit scarcity. This is not a governance promise — it is a protocol-level constraint.

4. **Community formation participation is natural.** Each lot is equal and public. There is no tiered allocation or reserved supply. This invites distributed formation across many participants.

5. **Durable identity and state history is valuable for an AI compute token.** Formation records cannot be backdated. Every token in existence has a traceable on-chain origin.

---

## What HACD adds

- **PoW container**: Each HACD was mined. The mining cost is a verifiable, sunk input that cannot be re-engineered or pre-arranged.
- **Formation cost**: 50 HAC per lot is a visible, on-chain cost that must be paid at formation time. This is not an administrative action — it is a protocol transaction.
- **Asset history**: Formed-at timestamps are permanent. Formation records cannot be altered, merged, or backdated.
- **Community formation**: 200 equal lots invite wide participation. No single participant controls all supply formation.

---

## Main Concerns

1. **Utility delivery is roadmap-dependent.** AI agent task infrastructure is not live at the time of issuance. Buyers of FRG are acquiring a future access claim, not a currently-active compute credit. This must be disclosed prominently and repeatedly.

2. **"1 FRG = 1 task" framing requires active infrastructure.** Until the AI agent layer is live, this statement describes a future state, not a current one. The demo should label this clearly.

3. **Stack cost language needs care.** The 50 HAC stack cost must never be described as a price floor, guaranteed redemption value, or backing mechanism. It is a formation input.

4. **No legal review completed.** For a project making utility claims tied to compute services, legal review is strongly recommended before public participation.
